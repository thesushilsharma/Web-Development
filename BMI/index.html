Your BMI

Underweight (BMI below 18.5)

May indicate malnutrition, eating disorders, or other health issues. Consider consulting a healthcare provider to discuss healthy weight gain strategies.

Healthy Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)

Associated with the lowest risk of weight-related health problems. Maintain this range through balanced nutrition and regular physical activity.

Note: For Asian populations, the healthy range is 18.5-22.9 due to different body composition and health risk profiles.

Overweight (BMI 25.0-29.9)

May increase risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and other health conditions. Consider lifestyle changes including diet and exercise.

Note: For Asian populations, overweight is classified as BMI 23.0-27.4.

Obese (BMI 30.0 and above)

Significantly increases risk of serious health conditions including heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and certain cancers. Medical consultation is strongly recommended.

Note: For Asian populations, obesity is classified as BMI 27.5 and above.
